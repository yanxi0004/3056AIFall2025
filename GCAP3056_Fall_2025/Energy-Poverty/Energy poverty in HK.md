## KEY ARGUMENTS FRAMEWORK

### **üéØ Main Thesis**
Hong Kong's universal Electricity Charges Relief Scheme (ECRS) contradicts the government's stated "targeted poverty alleviation" strategy and should be reformed into an efficiency-promoting, targeted system.

### **üèóÔ∏è Supporting Arguments**
1. **Policy Inconsistency**: ECRS universal approach vs. Commission on Poverty targeting mandate
2. **Ineffective Targeting**: High-income households receive same subsidy as energy-poor families
3. **Missed Opportunities**: No integration with energy efficiency programs (MEELS)
4. **Administrative Inefficiency**: Complex delivery system vs. simpler alternatives
5. **Subdivided Unit Gap**: Most vulnerable populations may not receive benefits

### **üí° Reform Proposals**
1. **Tiered Subsidy System**: Income-based eligibility and amounts
2. **Efficiency Incentives**: Rebates for energy-efficient appliances
3. **Direct Tenant Support**: Address subdivided unit landlord intermediation
4. **Policy Integration**: Combine ECRS with MEELS for comprehensive approach
