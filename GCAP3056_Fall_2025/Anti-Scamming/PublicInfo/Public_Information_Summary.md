# Public Information Summary: Anti-Scamming in Hong Kong

*This summary synthesizes research contributions from team members: Kevin (privacy & data security), Linda (user adoption & perception), Fion (technical functionality), Young (effectiveness metrics), and Arya (international comparison).*

## Executive Summary

Hong Kong faces a critical and escalating scam crisis, with 44,480 reported cases in 2024, representing an 11.7% increase from 2023 (39,824 cases). Despite government efforts through the "防騙視伏器" (Anti-Scam App) and various educational campaigns, the effectiveness of current measures remains questionable due to low public penetration, technological limitations, and design flaws that place excessive burden on users.

## Current Scam Landscape in Hong Kong

### Key Statistics (2024)
- **Total Cases**: 44,480 (up from 39,824 in 2023)
- **Financial Losses**: HK$9.15 billion (decreased by HK$30 million from 2023)
- **Growth Rate**: 12% increase in 2024 (significantly lower than 40%+ increases in 2022-2023)
- **Online Scams**: 61.8% of all cases (27,485 cases)

## Government Anti-Scam Measures

### Current Tools and Systems

#### 1. 防騙視伏器 (Anti-Scam App)
- **Downloads**: 96+ million (as of May 2025) *(Linda's research)*
- **Searches**: 8.4 million searches conducted
- **Alerts**: 1 million warnings issued
- **Functions**:
  - Suspicious call alerts
  - Website detection
  - Public reporting platform
  - Database verification

**Technical Integration (Fion's Research):**
- **FPS Integration**: Connected to 22,000+ suspicious FPS identifiers
- **Banking Integration**: Extended to online banking, counter transactions, and ATMs
- **Real-time Alerts**: 148,000+ alerts issued, preventing HK$180 million in high-risk transfers
- **Database Coverage**: 30,000+ blocked websites, 8,300+ blocked phone numbers

#### 2. Electronic Report Centre (e-RC)
- **Access**: Online, mobile app, in-person kiosks
- **Login**: Requires iAM Smart digital identity
- **Response Time**: 15-minute email confirmation
- **Support**: 24/7 helpline (8226 1886)

#### 3. Technical Measures
- **Blocked Websites**: 30,000+ suspicious links
- **Blocked Numbers**: 8,300+ phone numbers
- **Suspended Numbers**: 1.43 million local numbers
- **Call Blocking**: 5.7+ million calls blocked
- **Alerts Sent**: 30+ million voice/text alerts

### Recent Improvements
- **Customer Service Impersonation**: Reduced by 50% (from 1,100+ cases/month to ~500/month)
- **Financial Interception**: HK$1.48 billion intercepted in 2024
- **Arrests**: 10,496 people arrested (13.6% increase)
- **Money Laundering Prosecutions**: 1,484 cases (2.3x increase)

### Effectiveness Metrics (Young's Research)
**App Performance Indicators:**
- **Total Searches**: 7.24 million searches conducted
- **Alerts Issued**: 940,000 warnings to public
- **Public Reports**: 355,000+ suspicious calls and 38,000+ suspicious websites reported
- **User Behavior**: Despite warnings, some users still proceed with risky transactions
- **Growth Rate**: 2024 growth reduced to 12% (from 40%+ in 2022-2023)

## Critical Analysis: Limitations of Current Approach

### 1. Low Public Penetration (Linda's Research)
- **Download Rate**: 96+ million downloads (April 2025: 90 million, July 2025: 100 million)
- **Social Media Engagement**: Facebook (67,000 followers), Instagram (8,384 followers)
- **Active Usage**: Limited evidence of consistent user engagement
- **User Feedback**: Mixed reviews on app stores, with some users reporting technical issues

### 2. User-Dependent Design Flaws
- **Manual Activation Required**: Users must proactively check suspicious contacts
- **No Automatic Protection**: Unlike Singapore's ScamShield, no silent blocking
- **Vulnerable Populations**: Elderly and digitally illiterate users at highest risk

### 3. Technological Limitations (Fion's Research)
- **Static Database**: Relies on known bad actors list vs. AI-powered detection
- **Platform Restriction**: Mobile-only, ignoring desktop/laptop threats
- **Limited Scope**: Cannot scan WhatsApp, Telegram, or websites like Singapore's system
- **Update Frequency**: Database updates lack transparency; app updates (20+ in 3 years) without detailed change logs
- **User Experience Issues**: Complex interface, browser popup windows for core functions, language inconsistency

### 4. Transparency Issues
- **Update Logs**: Frequent updates (20+ in 3 years) without detailed change descriptions
- **Missing Metrics**: No published false-positive/negative rates
- **Performance Data**: Lack of outcome statistics for reported cases

### 5. Privacy and Data Security (Kevin's Research)
**Hong Kong vs. Singapore Privacy Approaches:**
- **Hong Kong**: Local processing, no data upload to cloud, better privacy protection
- **Singapore**: Cloud-based processing, more data collection and sharing
- **Hong Kong Advantage**: Privacy policy ensures data stays on device, no personal information saved
- **Singapore Limitation**: Requires extensive permissions, collects device information and usage patterns

### 6. User Experience Problems
- **Brand Confusion**: Multiple names (防騙視伏器, CyberDefender, Anti-Scam App)
- **Complex Interface**: Overly complicated UI compared to competitors
- **Reporting Barriers**: Mandatory text descriptions required vs. screenshot-only options

## Policy Recommendations

### Immediate Actions
1. **Implement Automated Protection**: Partner with telecom providers for silent call/SMS filtering
2. **Invest in AI Technology**: Move beyond static databases to dynamic threat detection
3. **Expand Platform Coverage**: Develop browser extensions and cross-platform solutions
4. **Enhance Transparency**: Publish performance metrics and detailed update logs
5. **Improve User Experience**: Unify branding and streamline interface design

### Long-term Strategy
- **State-led Integration**: Move from citizen self-defense to government-provided digital security
- **Proactive Measures**: Implement Singapore-style automated blocking systems
- **Cross-platform Protection**: Extend coverage beyond mobile devices
- **Public Trust Building**: Through transparent operations and performance reporting

## Conclusion

While Hong Kong's anti-scam efforts show some positive trends (reduced growth rates, successful interception of funds), the current approach remains fundamentally flawed. The heavy reliance on user vigilance, technological limitations, and poor public engagement create significant gaps in protection. A comprehensive overhaul focusing on automated, AI-powered, and user-friendly solutions is essential to effectively combat the evolving scam landscape.

The comparison with Singapore's ScamShield demonstrates that more effective solutions are possible through proactive government intervention, advanced technology integration, and user-centered design. Hong Kong must evolve from symbolic measures to substantive, integrated digital security systems to truly protect its citizens from the growing threat of cyber-enabled fraud.
